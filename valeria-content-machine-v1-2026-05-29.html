Tone of Voice · needs Val OK

Strategic challenger with smart simplicity

Это текущая версия voice, которую нужно подтвердить на реальных примерах. Это не “быть злой” и не “быть милой”. Это звучать как человек, который быстро видит слабое место в learning / transformation системе и умеет объяснить его простым бизнес-языком.

Voice statement

Val sounds commercially sharp, evidence-aware, practical and slightly contrarian. She challenges assumptions, removes corporate fluff and turns complexity into clear learning and adoption decisions.
Dimension Use Do not use Example direction
Commercial sharpness Business consequence, adoption risk, execution cost, performance signal. Soft reflection with no stakes. “If the new process is slower in real work, people will not adopt it because the slide deck was clear.”
Evidence-aware Observed patterns, research, field notes, examples, credible sources. Big guru claims and unsupported “truths”. “The workaround is not noise. It is adoption data.”
Practical Diagnostic questions, checklists, design decisions, better alternatives. Empty inspiration and “leaders should care more”. “Before building another course, decide what belongs in training and what belongs in the workflow.”
Slightly contrarian Challenge learning theater, order-taking, launch obsession, fake clarity. Provocation as performance, anti-HR irritation, personal attacks. “People do not always resist change. Often, they resist unclear work.”
Smart simplicity Short logic, clean definitions, plain language for complex enterprise work. Corporate буль-бульк, jargon fog, AI-perfect paragraphs. “More information is not more clarity. Clarity helps people act.”

Writing formula

Sharp observation
-> business consequence
-> learning / performance-support mechanism
-> practical alternative
-> one clean takeaway

What Val sends before Monday

Val Deep Research Brief · paste into Codex

You are Val's LinkedIn research partner.

Goal:
Help Val become more visible on LinkedIn as a business-minded learning strategist who helps organizations use learning to turn complex change into new ways of working people actually adopt.

Research topic:
[PASTE TOPIC]

Val's positioning:
Val combines learning strategy, consulting thinking, marketing, and transformation experience to translate business complexity into practical learning and adoption systems.

Val's content pillars:
1. Making Change Work
- Human reality of change after launch: overwhelm, workarounds, uneven manager support, old habits, adoption friction.
- Learning mechanism: learning teams use these insights to design support that helps people adopt new ways of working.

2. Making Complexity Usable
- Smart simplicity: turning systems, processes, rules, ownership and exceptions into usable action.
- Learning mechanism: learning and performance support help people move from understanding change to doing the work differently.

3. Creative Strategy for Change
- Positioning, story, Big Idea, message and relevance for internal transformation.
- Learning mechanism: learning is not just information transfer; it helps people understand why change matters and what to do differently.

Reference voices and lenses:
- Josh Bersin: research-backed HR / L&D / workforce synthesis. Use for trends, evidence, market language.
- Pavel Bezruchko: smart simplicity in management. Use for clean definitions, practical logic, no-water explanation.
- David James: business-first L&D, outcomes over order-taking. Use for performance and business-problem framing.
- April Dunford: positioning discipline. Use audience, alternatives, value, proof, and why-this-matters framing.
- Szilvia Olah: sharp anti-theater pattern naming. Use carefully for edge, not for grievance or anti-HR tone.

Val's voice:
Commercially sharp, evidence-aware, practical, slightly contrarian, challenges assumptions, no learning theater, no empty inspiration, no corporate fluff.

Do not write the LinkedIn post yet.
First build a research brief.

Return:

1. Topic fit
- Which content pillar this belongs to.
- Why this topic matters for L&D / learning strategy, not just change management.
- What business problem sits underneath it.

2. Source map
- 5-8 credible sources, reports, podcasts, articles, company examples or expert perspectives to inspect.
- For each source: why it is useful, what claim it may support, what to be careful with.

3. Reference voice lenses
- What would the Josh Bersin lens notice?
- What would the Pavel Bezruchko smart-simplicity lens clarify?
- What would the David James business-outcomes lens challenge?
- What would the April Dunford positioning lens sharpen?
- What would the Szilvia Olah anti-theater lens name directly, but safely?

4. Tensions and contrarian angles
- 5 tensions in the topic.
- 5 assumptions worth challenging.
- 3 angles that are sharp but still corporate-safe.

5. Learning mechanism
- What belongs in training?
- What belongs in performance support / workflow support?
- What belongs with managers?
- What belongs in communication / positioning?
- What should not be solved by another course?

6. Field examples
- 3 anonymized case-style situations Val could write about.
- For each: situation -> friction -> business consequence -> learning/adoption decision -> lesson.

7. Risk check
- Claims that need verification before publishing.
- Words that may be too broad or risky.
- Where the evidence is strong, weak or only anecdotal.

8. Post opportunities
- 5 LinkedIn post angles.
- For each: hook, core argument, learning mechanism, practical takeaway, best format.

9. Voice guardrails
- Phrases that sound like Val.
- Phrases that sound generic / too nice / too harsh / too AI.
- One rewrite rule to make the topic sound more like Val.

10. Exa search queries
- 10 search queries for Exa that would find better sources, people, examples or reports for this topic.

Monday pipeline

Search -> Strategy -> Voice -> Humanizer -> De-AI -> Do-ification -> Drafts

Это финальная точка менторства: не “мы поговорили про LinkedIn”, а у Леры есть процесс, который можно повторять каждую неделю.

Step What happens Output Pass / fail
1 · Search ready Exa + Deep Research по одной теме или одному pillar. Research brief, sources, tensions, examples, post angles. Есть минимум 3 credible sources and one real business tension.
2 · Strategy check needs OK Проверка на positioning, pillar fit, audience, learning mechanism. Green / yellow / red topic decision. Если нет learning mechanism, тема не идет в draft.
3 · Voice rewrite needs OK Черновик переписывается в Val voice: sharp, practical, evidence-aware. Draft 1 in Val direction. Звучит как learning strategist with edge, not generic LinkedIn.
4 · Humanizer ready Добавляем живую логику, наблюдение, естественный rhythm, field language. Draft 2 with human reasoning. Есть lived observation, not just polished explanation.
5 · De-AI ready Убираем AI structure, fake drama, guru cadence, over-clean transitions. Draft 3, less synthetic. Нет “In today's fast-paced world”, empty lists, fake cliffhangers.
6 · Do-ification ready Превращаем мысль в полезное действие: diagnostic question, design decision, checklist, next step. Action-oriented draft. После текста понятно, что с этим делать в learning / transformation work.
7 · Drafts folder Сохраняем финальный черновик, research brief, hooks and notes. Drafts library for publishing and voice training. Можно вернуться через месяц и понять, почему текст написан именно так.
